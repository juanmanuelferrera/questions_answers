Internal variations include moderate Maturidis emphasizing robust secondary causation, conservative Maturidis approaching Ash'ari occasionalism, and rationalist Maturidis developing philosophical integration. Hanafi legal school typically adopts Maturidi theology. Tensions with strict Ash'ari occasionalism continue.

Counter-arguments address concerns that secondary causation diminishes divine sovereignty and that emphasizing human agency approaches Mu'tazilite rationalism. Maturidis respond that affirming created causal powers manifests rather than limits divine wisdom and that genuine moral responsibility requires real human capacity.

Contemporary applications include Turkish Diyanet maintaining Maturidi theology, Hanafi institutions teaching moderate occasionalism, and Muslim engagement with scientific causation. Maturidi temporal understanding provides Sunni theological alternative emphasizing rational intelligibility.

Comparative note: Maturidi moderate occasionalism balancing divine sovereignty and secondary causation occupies middle position between Ash'ari strict occasionalism eliminating natural causation and Mu'tazilite rationalism emphasizing creaturely autonomy, resembling Thomistic primary-secondary causation while maintaining stronger divine determination.

*** 1.6.30 Sunni Islam (Athari/Salafi) Response

Time exists according to revealed texts requiring submission without rationalistic speculation, with temporal understanding derived exclusively from Qur'an and authentic Sunnah according to salaf methodology rejecting philosophical and theological innovation (bid'ah) while affirming divine attributes including eternity (qidam) without metaphorical interpretation (ta'wil). Athari temporal understanding emerges from textualist tradition resisting kalam speculation and philosophical temporal theories.

Historical development begins with early hadith scholars including Ahmad ibn Hanbal (780-855 CE) rejecting Mu'tazilite rationalism during mihna persecution. Ibn Taymiyyah (1263-1328) articulated comprehensive Athari theology critiquing both philosophers and theologians, affirming divine attributes literally while rejecting anthropomorphism (tajsim) and affirming incomparability (tanzih). Ibn Qayyim al-Jawziyya developed Ibn Taymiyyah's temporal theology. Muhammad ibn Abd al-Wahhab (1703-1792) founded Wahhabi movement emphasizing tawhid and rejecting innovations. Contemporary Salafism draws on Athari tradition through figures including Nasiruddin al-Albani emphasizing hadith authentication and Ibn Baz maintaining strict textualism.

Key foundations include textual literalism (zahir) accepting divine attributes without metaphorical interpretation, rejection of kalam speculation including occasionalism, affirmation of divine temporal will without philosophizing, and commitment to salaf methodology. Athari temporal theology emphasizes divine revelation's sufficiency without rational elaboration.

Core arguments include: (1) Time's nature requires revealed understanding without philosophical speculation introducing innovation; (2) Divine attributes including eternity affirmed literally according to textual meaning; (3) Temporal processes understood through revealed texts without occasionalist or rationalist theories. Qur'an 57:3 affirms Allah as First and Last, 55:29 describes divine creative activity, and authentic hadith provide temporal understanding without philosophical elaboration requiring simple acceptance.

Internal variations include strict Wahhabis rejecting all theological speculation, moderate Atharis accepting limited rational demonstration, and contemporary Salafis emphasizing hadith methodology. Some Atharis accept aspects of Ash'ari theology while others strictly reject kalam. Tensions between textualism and rational engagement continue.

Counter-arguments address concerns that Athari literalism produces anthropomorphic understanding and that rejecting rational theology limits intellectual engagement. Atharis respond that revealed texts provide adequate temporal understanding without rationalistic distortion and that philosophical speculation introduces innovation corrupting pure Islamic temporality.

Contemporary applications include Salafi institutions teaching textual methodology, Saudi religious establishment maintaining Athari creed, and conservative Sunni resistance to liberal Islamic temporal reinterpretation. Athari temporal understanding provides scripturalist alternative to theological speculation.

Comparative note: Athari textualist temporal understanding rejecting philosophical and theological speculation contrasts both Ash'ari occasionalism and Maturidi rationalism while resembling Christian fundamentalist biblicism, prioritizing revealed temporal understanding over rational elaboration while claiming authentic Islamic orthodoxy.

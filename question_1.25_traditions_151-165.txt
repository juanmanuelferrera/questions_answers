Modal metaphysics rejected as unverifiable metaphysics—no empirical import, scientifically sterile.

*** 1.25.153 Quine's Naturalized Epistemology Response

Counterfactuals pragmatically useful but philosophically obscure: no objective fact determining which counterfactual conditionals true. Holism: cannot isolate mathematical claims' modal status from total theoretical web. Naturalism: epistemology continuous with empirical science; modal metaphysics scientifically dispensable.

*** 1.25.154 Modal Realism (Lewis) Response

Lewis's modal realism approaches possible worlds as concrete realities: possible worlds exist as concrete, causally-isolated, spatiotemporally unified maximal sums of spatiotemporally related objects—just like this actual world but disconnected. Counterfactuals analyze via similarity: "if P, Q" true if Q holds at closest P-worlds. Mathematical possibilities concern abstract structures instantiated across worlds. Necessity = truth in all worlds; possibility = truth in some world. Modal realism ontologically expensive but theoretically powerful—systematic semantics for modality, counterfactuals, properties (classes of possibilia). This world one among countless equally real alternatives; "actual" merely reflects our spatiotemporal location, not metaphysical privilege.

*** 1.25.156 Nominalism (Field) Response

*** 1.25.159 Intuitionism (Brouwer) Response

*** 1.17.45 EVANGELICAL (FUNDAMENTALIST) Response

Time is unquestionably real, established by God's creative fiat during the literal six twenty-four-hour days of creation week approximately six thousand years ago, providing the historical framework within which Scripture's inerrant prophecies find precise fulfillment and sinners face urgent decision before death or rapture terminates opportunity for salvation through personal faith in Christ's substitutionary atonement. Fundamentalist Christianity, defined by the five fundamentals (biblical inerrancy, Christ's deity, virgin birth, substitutionary atonement, bodily resurrection and return) and militant opposition to theological liberalism and cultural compromise, understands time not as evolutionary eons or mystical illusion but as recent, linear history recorded accurately in Scripture and hastening toward prophesied consummation.

Young-earth creationism, the predominant Fundamentalist position, affirms that Genesis 1-11 records literal history occurring within recent chronology. Archbishop Ussher's calculation dating creation to 4004 BC, while not doctrinally binding, represents reasonable inference from biblical genealogies. This chronology conflicts dramatically with evolutionary geology's billions-of-years framework, yet Fundamentalists maintain that God created mature earth with appearance of age—light from distant stars reaching earth instantly, Adam created adult, trees possessing rings. This requires time's reality while compressing its duration radically compared to secular science.

The pretribulational, premillennial rapture theology anticipates Christ's imminent, any-moment return to catch away true believers before seven-year tribulation, during which Antichrist rules and God's wrath judges rebellious humanity. This requires temporal sequence—rapture, tribulation, Christ's visible return, millennial kingdom, final judgment, eternal state. Prophecy teachers chart these events with precision, identifying contemporary geopolitical developments (Israeli statehood, European Union, microchip technology) as fulfilling specific biblical predictions. This prophetic urgency presupposes time's reality and acceleration toward divinely appointed climax.

The emphasis on personal conversion through conscious decision—walking the aisle, praying the sinner's prayer, inviting Jesus into one's heart—marks a definite moment separating lost past from saved present. Fundamentalist testimonies invariably specify when one got saved, distinguishing clearly between pre-conversion darkness and post-conversion light. This binary temporal division requires time's reality as the dimension where eternal destinies are determined through punctiliar decisions.

Biblical inerrancy in original autographs means Scripture's historical claims—creation, flood, exodus, conquest, Jesus' miracles, resurrection—describe actual events occurring within real chronological sequence. Fundamentalists vigorously defend biblical historicity against critical scholarship's attempts to reinterpret miracles as myths or legends. Time's reality makes historical verification meaningful and apologetic defense necessary.

The urgency of evangelistic preaching—"now is the day of salvation" (2 Corinthians 6:2)—recognizes that physical death or Christ's return terminates opportunity for conversion. Hell's eternal conscious torment awaits those who die unsaved, making evangelism literally life-or-death temporal urgency requiring aggressive soul-winning before time expires.
